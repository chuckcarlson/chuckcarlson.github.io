Verizon in Minecraft


Verizon is an expert in building their network in hard-to-get places. To demonstrate this, we extended the Verizon network into the world of Minecraft. Players could create cell phones to connect with the real world. They could video chat with friends, send selfies, browse the web, and even order pizza, all without leaving the game.

2016 — Art Director — Gold Cyber Lion

Beneath The Surface


To show the world that Chrysler can make a world-class car, we allowed customers to go “beneath the surface” of the all-new Chrysler 200. Using VR, viewers sat inside an actual 200 and watched as the car's parts exploded around them. They could then dive-in deeper by transporting onto the factory line and learn about the build process as the car moved along the line.

2015 — Art Director

Door


Have you ever been in a crowded stadium with full bars of service, but your phone still doesn't work? That's where a better network comes into play. Using a metaphor of a door, the spot visualized what actually happens when a network becomes too busy and how Verizon better handles it.

2015 — Art Director

City


Because a cellular network is invisible, it's nearly impossible to show all the amazing features it can have. Using a city as a metaphor, we created an interactive website that allowed visitors to understand the inner-workings of what makes the Verizon network the best.

2016 — Art Director

Little Metaphors


Extending alongside our campaign of "better matters" for Verizon, we continued the use of metaphors with a series of digital :15s. The spots featured a basketball hoop, a long straw, an hourglass, and a lizard to help explain what makes Verizon the best.

2015 — Art Director

Dodge / Burgundy


To launch the all-new Dodge Durango we wanted to show the world that its a pretty big deal. With the release of the truck coinciding with the release of Anchorman 2, we brought in America's favorite fictional newsanchor, Ron Burgundy, to help deliver the goods through a ton of spots.

2014 — Art Director

Hands on Ron Burgundy


To extend the Anchorman 2 and Dodge Durango campaign, we created an internet endurance event inspired by the car dealership contest "hands on a hardbody." We invited users to compete online to see how long they could touch Ron for a chance to win a Dodge Durango.

2014 — Art Director

Rooftop Racer


One of my favorite projects I made while working at CP+B was an interactive game where Elliot Sadler leaves his Coke Zero on the roof of his car. The goal for the user was to try to finish the race with the Coke Zero intact. When this game released, it really pushed the boundaries of what a browser game could look like, by delivering next-gen console like graphics.

2008 — Art Director, Designer
